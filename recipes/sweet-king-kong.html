Sweet king kong

What is this dish about?

The Peruvian King Kong is a sweet recognized as a heritage of the city of Lambayeque in the northern coastal region of the country. It is a giant 4-layer cookie alfajor filled with pineapple candy, pot delicacy and peanut candy. A traditional dessert that combines crunchy cookies that melt in your mouth and is wrapped with fillings that have flavors between citrus, dairy and caramel.

Ingredients to make King Kong candy:

  • 330 gramos de harina
  • 1 pizca de levadura seca instantánea
  • 2 cucharadas soperas de ajo molido
  • 1 pizca de sal
  • 95 gramos de agua
  • 80 gramos de mantequilla

    • Para el dulce de piña:

  • 300 gramos de piña
  • 100 gramos de azucar (½ taza)

    • Para el manjar blanco de olla:

  • 1 lata de leche evaporada
  • 400 gramos de azúcar (2 tazas)
  • ½ lata de leche condensada

    • Para el dulce de maní:

  • 2 camotes medianos
  • 50 gramos de miel
  • 150 gramos de chancaca rallada o picada finamente
  • 100 gramos de maní tostado

How to make King Kong candy:

Start by preparing the cookie. In a bowl she pours the flour, a pinch of yeast and salt. Incorporate the liquid ingredients which are the yolks and water. Start kneading, you will notice that it comes together into a dry dough.

Next, add the butter cube by cube. Continue kneading until you obtain a soft and homogeneous ball of dough. If you want to speed up the process you can do it with the help of a stand mixer with the bread hook.

After the resting time has elapsed, stretch the dough into a rectangular shape until it is approximately half a finger thick.

Place the dough on a plate with a silicone mat or parchment paper and proceed to prick it, this way it will grow evenly without many air bubbles.

Divide it into 4 equal parts, but don't cut it yet, just mark it. Bake at 150 °C and after 15 minutes remove the plate and proceed to cut along the marks. Return the plate and continue baking for a further 30 minutes until the surface is lightly golden. Reserve at room temperature.

Separately, prepare the pineapple candy. To make it, first wash, peel, remove the core and cut the pineapple into small cubes, if you wish you can also grate it.

Next, place it in a saucepan and add the sugar. Turn the heat to minimum so that the liquid evaporates slowly and a very dense pineapple jam is obtained. Remember to stir sporadically to prevent the base from burning and maintain the bright yellow color. Booking.

For the pot blancmange, in another medium saucepan, pour the milk, sugar and condensed milk. Bring to the heat and mix well. Let it come to a boil and continue stirring until it thickens. When you notice that a growing foam is forming, move quickly or remove it from the heat for a couple of seconds so that it does not overflow. When 30 minutes have passed, it will look like a lighter dulce de leche, so remove from heat and let cool.

Finally, prepare the peanut candy. Start by washing, peeling, and thickly slicing the sweet potatoes. Place them in another saucepan, cover with hot water and let them cook until soft.

When the sweet potatoes are cooked through, strain and mash them to obtain a silky puree.

Pour this puree into a saucepan and add the chancaca, honey and a splash of water until it covers.

Let it cook until the chancaca is completely melted and looks like a dense dark brown paste. Remember to stir to avoid burning.

To finish, add pieces of peanuts or peanut butter to maintain the silky texture.

Assemble the dessert, that is, put a cookie as a base, spread the peanut candy on top, cover with another cookie, fill with the delicacy and cover again with another cookie.

Spread the pineapple candy and cover with a cookie. Each layer of filling should be about 1cm thick.

Finally, fill and smooth the sides. Cut, serve and enjoy! Tell us your opinion in the comments and share with us a photograph of the final result.
